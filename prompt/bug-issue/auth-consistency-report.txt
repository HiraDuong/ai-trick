Finding 1
Category: Backend stricter than shared studio policy
Area: Unpublished article engagement and stats access
Files:
- apps/backend/src/services/comment.service.ts
- apps/backend/src/services/reaction.service.ts
- apps/backend/src/services/helpfulness.service.ts
- apps/backend/src/services/stats.service.ts
- apps/backend/src/utils/studio-access.utils.ts

Observed behavior
- Core article management and draft detail in apps/backend/src/services/article.service.ts uses shared studio rules: AUTHOR or EDITOR may enter studio flows, and EDITOR may manage any article through canManageArticle().
- Comment, reaction, helpfulness, and stats services still use AUTHOR-only unpublished access checks of the form user.role !== "AUTHOR" || user.id !== article.authorId.

Impact
- EDITOR is allowed to manage unpublished content in article and version services, but the same EDITOR is blocked from draft-adjacent endpoints on the same unpublished article.
- This is a real authorization inconsistency inside the backend and can surface as partial access if draft preview or management tooling expands.

Recommendation
- For unpublished articles, replace duplicated AUTHOR-only checks with shared studio-aware ownership logic based on hasStudioAccessRole() and canManageArticle().

Finding 3
Category: Latent frontend/backend contract mismatch
Area: Public article page versus unpublished access
Files:
- apps/frontend/src/app/articles/[articleId]/page.tsx
- apps/frontend/src/lib/api.ts
- apps/backend/src/services/article.service.ts

Observed behavior
- The public article page always prefetches detail, comments, helpfulness, stats, and reactions through anonymous-capable helpers in apps/frontend/src/lib/api.ts.
- Backend article detail already allows AUTHOR or EDITOR access to unpublished articles when authenticated, but the public page does not provide an authenticated draft preview flow.

Impact
- This is not currently a user-facing security bug because there is no frontend route promising editor draft preview outside studio.
- It is a contract gap: backend supports authenticated unpublished article detail, while the current frontend public article route is effectively published-only.

Recommendation
- Keep the public article route published-only.
- If draft preview is required, add a dedicated authenticated studio detail flow instead of extending the public page implicitly.
